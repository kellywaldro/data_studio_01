A trip to the pharmacy.

​

The pharmacy in France is ubiquitous. Small, independent and identifiable by a bright LED green cross. It is in every way a window onto French culture: the prescriptions that are almost entirely covered by the public healthcare system, the unscented skincare products, and the opening times which read "fermé le Dimanche" (closed on Sunday).

It hadn't occurred to me just how many there were until I was standing at the view point on top of l'Arc de Triomphe in Paris last month and noticed the bright green lights scattered across the city.
